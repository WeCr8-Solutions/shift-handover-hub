## Part 2 — `10-purple-cow.md` (the breakout campaign)

### Recommended flagship: **"The Machinist" — an annual flagship media property**

A multi-format editorial product built around the people the trade has forgotten to celebrate. Three components, one campaign, annual cadence:

1. **The Machinist Portrait Project** — commission a top-tier documentary photographer to portrait 100 American machinists each year, sourced from the JobLine talent roster. Released as:
   - A free hardcover coffee-table book (limited print run; library/museum/shop distribution).
   - A free web gallery with operator bios, machines run, credentials.
   - A traveling exhibit (Smithsonian / regional manufacturing museums / IMTS floor / partner trade schools).

2. **State-of-the-Floor Report** — annual data-driven report sourced from anonymized platform data: shift-handoff completion, credential adoption, shop-floor productivity benchmarks. Published free, designed to be the citation everyone in the trade and the press reaches for. Think *Stripe's State of Developers* or *Mary Meeker's Internet Trends*, for US precision machining.

3. **Founder long-form essay collection** — bundled with the book. The "why this matters" arc, on the record, in the founder's voice.

### Why this is the right purple cow for JobLine

- **Remarkable** (Godin's bar): nobody has ever done this for machinists. The press will write about *that fact alone*.
- **On-brand**: it is literally the brand promise made physical — "we make shop-floor work visible, portable, and respected."
- **Compounding**: annual release builds an archive. Year 5 is more valuable than year 1.
- **Citable**: photos + data + names = the artifacts trade press, mainstream press, and AI answer engines all cite for years.
- **Bridges out of the niche** without leaving it: museums, design press, *The Atlantic*, *NYT Magazine*, *Bloomberg*, *60 Minutes*-grade longform are all reachable from this product. None require us to dilute brand voice.
- **Serves both sides**: operators become heroes (supply moat); shops become destinations to be photographed at (demand magnet).
- **Hard to copy**: requires the credentialed roster + platform data. A competitor without our supply side can't replicate it.
- **Founder-led**, fits doc 01.

### Two secondary "purple cow" bets the file will also document

- **Operator Trading Cards** — physical, beautifully designed cards of top credentialed operators with stats. Distributed at shows, in shops, online. Reverent and funny at once; social-native. Cheap. Earns its own press cycle.
- **The Expeditor Index** — a public, Bloomberg-style index of US shop-floor productivity sourced from anonymized JobLine data. CNBC-tickerable; press-citable; positions us as the data authority. Long fuse, big payoff.

The file will name these as **deliberate, additive bets** with budget caps, success criteria, and kill rules — not "while we're at it" extras.

### What the file will explicitly NOT propose

- Stunt PR (giant inflatable mill on the side of a freeway, celebrity endorsements, Super Bowl spots).
- Anything that violates brand-debt rules in doc 01 (no surveillance framing, no AI-as-headline, no punching down).
- Anything that requires ITAR-controlled photography or data.
- Anything that depends on a single moment instead of compounding annually.
