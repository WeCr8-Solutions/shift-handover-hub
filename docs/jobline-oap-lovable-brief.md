## WHAT THE OAP PROGRAM IS

The JobLine OAP is a structured, section-by-section onboarding program for manufacturing operators — from their first day on the shop floor through final employer certification on specific machines.

It is not just a training app. It is a **credentialing system** — one that creates portable, verifiable certificates that operators can carry from job to job, and that employers can trust because they issued them.

### The 7-Section OAP Flow Every Operator Moves Through:

1. **Company Orientation** — HR, policies, facility tour, emergency procedures
2. **Safety & EHS** — PPE, Lockout/Tagout, HazCom, fire extinguisher, OSHA fundamentals
3. **Material Handling** — Raw material ID, certifications, saw operation, lifting, traceability
4. **Measurement & Inspection** — Tape, caliper, micrometer, bore gauge, thread gauges, print reading
5. **Tooling & Preset** — End mills, drills, taps, inserts, holders, workholding, preset station
6. **Machine Qualification** — Machine-specific OAK checklist (CNC Lathe, VMC, HMC, Swiss, Grinder, Saw, CMM)
7. **Floor Certification** — Final process checklists, quality standards, employer buy-off

Each section has:
- Course content that teaches the material
- Quiz questions that test comprehension
- Checkpoints that a mentor must sign off
- Some checkpoints that require employer final buy-off
- Mentee notes fields throughout
- Progress tracking visible to both mentor and employer

## THE TWO AUDIENCES AND WHAT THEY NEED

### Audience 1 — The Individual Operator (B2C)

This is a machinist, CNC operator, material handler, inspector, or anyone entering or advancing in manufacturing. They earn $18–$38/hour. They pay out of pocket — they cannot expense-report anything. They want one thing from us: **a credential they can bring to a job interview or add to their resume that proves they know what they are doing.**

They do not care about the platform. They care about the certificate.

They will use our platform in two scenarios:

**Scenario A — Standalone (no employer):** They find us online, take courses on their own, pass the quizzes, and generate a self-certified portable certificate. They bring this to job interviews to demonstrate initiative and base competency. An employer can use this as a starting point and run them through final verifications rather than starting from zero.

**Scenario B — Employer-enrolled:** Their employer is already using JobLine OAP. The employer enrolls them, assigns a mentor, and walks them through the full structured program. The employer issues a stamped certificate at the end.

### Audience 2 — The Employer (B2B)

This is a shop owner, operations manager, quality engineer, or HR director at a manufacturing company — anywhere from a 5-person job shop to a 500-person Tier 1 aerospace supplier. They have budget. They have compliance pressure. They have turnover problems. They have customer audits that require documented training records.

They need:
- A place to build their company profile and define their OAP requirements per role
- A program builder where they select which machines, measuring tools, tooling, and safety certifications are required for each job role they hire for
- A mentor management system — who is authorized to train and sign off on what
- An operator enrollment and tracking dashboard — where each person is in the program, what is complete, what is pending
- Digital sign-off and buy-off workflow replacing paper binders
- Certificate issuance with their company name, the operator's name, machines qualified, equipment, and dates
- Compliance-ready export for audits (AS9100, OSHA, customer audits)

## THE CHECKOUT FLOW WE NEED BUILT

The flow is simple and must have near-zero friction:

1. Operator completes courses and passes quizzes (tracked in the app)
2. They click "Get My Certificate" — a button visible once they pass a minimum threshold
3. They see a preview of their certificate — their name, courses passed, date, cert ID
4. A single-screen checkout appears:
   - Name field (pre-filled if they entered it earlier)
   - Email field (for delivery)
   - Payment: $12 one-time via Stripe
   - Accepted: Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Apple Pay, Google Pay
5. After payment:
   - Instant certificate PDF generation
   - Email delivery with PDF attachment
   - Shareable link generated (unique URL they can put on LinkedIn)
   - QR code visible on the certificate that resolves to a verification page
6. No account required to purchase — guest checkout is acceptable
7. Optional: create a free account to store and re-access the certificate later

The certificate must look professional. It is the product. It needs the WeCr8/JobLine branding, the operator's name large and prominent, a list of courses and machines qualified, the issue date, an expiry date if applicable, a unique certificate ID, and space for employer and mentor signatures if issued through an employer account.

## THE CERTIFICATE — WHAT IT MUST CONTAIN

The certificate is the core deliverable. It must be taken seriously as a physical document people will print, laminate, scan, and attach to job applications. It is not a participation ribbon. It is a professional credential.

**Required elements on every certificate:**

- JobLine OAP logo and program name
- "CERTIFICATE OF QUALIFICATION" as the headline
- Operator's full name — large, prominent, center of the document
- The role they were qualified for (e.g., CNC Lathe Operator, Mill Setup Technician)
- Issuing employer name and city (or "Self-Certified / JobLine OAP" for standalone)
- Machines they are qualified to operate — listed individually
- Measuring equipment they have been signed off on
- Tooling competencies
- Safety certifications completed
- Issue date
- Expiration date (if applicable — employer sets this, default 2 years)
- Unique certificate ID (alphanumeric, e.g., CERT-A3F2K1-2026)
- QR code that resolves to a verification URL
- Mentor name and signature line
- Employer/authorized signer name and signature line
- Operator name and signature line
- Portability note: "This certificate is portable and may be presented to any employer"
- "Issued through the JobLine Operator Acceptance Program — jobline.ai"

**Design requirements:**
- Portrait orientation, standard 8.5x11 print-ready
- Dark, professional manufacturing aesthetic — not a generic school certificate
- Heavy typography, bold name treatment
- Equipment list displayed as clean tags/pills
- Must look credible next to a welding cert or forklift card
- Print-to-PDF from browser without any scaling issues

## THE VERIFICATION PAGE

Every certificate has a unique ID and QR code. When an employer scans the QR code or enters the cert ID at a verification URL (e.g., jobline.ai/verify/CERT-A3F2K1-2026), they see:

- Operator name
- Role qualified for
- Issuing employer (or self-certified)
- Issue date and expiration date
- Machines and equipment qualified on
- Safety certifications completed
- Certificate status: VALID / EXPIRED / REVOKED
- Issued through JobLine OAP

This page is public and requires no login. It exists purely to let employers verify a certificate that an operator brings to an interview.

## LONG-TERM VISION (BUILD TOWARD THIS)

The end state is not a training app. The end state is **the industry-standard credential for manufacturing operators** — the way a CDL is the standard for truck drivers or NIMS credentials are recognized in CNC machining.

To get there:
1. Build the employer base (50–100 shops using the program) through the subscription model
2. Standardize the OAK checkpoints with trade associations (AMT, NTMA, PMPA)
3. Partner with community colleges and CTEs — OAP as a stackable credential (WIOA ETPL eligible)
4. The certificate becomes worth $35–75 as a recognized industry credential, not $12
5. Employers begin writing "JobLine OAP certified preferred" on job postings
6. Operators complete OAP before applying, not after getting hired

That flywheel — operators getting certified because employers ask for it, employers asking for it because operators arrive certified — is the business.
