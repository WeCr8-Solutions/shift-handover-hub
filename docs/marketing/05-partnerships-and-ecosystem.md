# 05 — Partnerships & Ecosystem

## Goal

Borrow trust we haven't yet earned by aligning with organizations the trade already trusts. Partnerships are how an unknown brand reaches a skeptical audience without paying a per-click tax.

## Partnership philosophy

- **Trust > volume.** One endorsement from NTMA outweighs 100,000 ad impressions.
- **Value flows both ways or it doesn't work.** Don't pitch partners on what they can do for us. Pitch on what we can do for *their* members.
- **Slow > fast.** Real partnerships compound across years. Treat them as relationships, not deals.
- **Founder-led until there's a partnership lead.** No partnership can be outsourced until it's repeatable.

## Partner categories

### Tier 1 — Tool & metrology vendors (presetting, measurement, calibration)

- **ZOLLER**, Hexagon, Mitutoyo, Mahr, Renishaw, Marposs. These vendors visit ICP shops weekly. Their reps are the trade's most-trusted advisors after the shop's own veterans.
- **Posture**: Integrate where it makes sense (tool data import from presetters, measurement results capture for OAP). Co-author content. Be in their booth at IMTS, not the other way around.
- **What they get**: their data becomes part of the operator credential and the shift handoff. Their reps look smart recommending us.
- **What we get**: warm intros into every shop they already serve.

### Tier 2 — ERP vendors and resellers

- **JobBOSS, ProShop, E2, Global Shop, SAP**, plus the regional implementation firms that resell them. We already have JobBOSS and SAP connector scaffolding (see ERP memory entries).
- **Posture**: "We complete you, we don't replace you." Read-through default mode (ITAR-safe) means we never ask the ERP vendor to hand us their customer's system of record.
- **What they get**: a shop-floor surface they don't have to build. Better stickiness for their core ERP.
- **What we get**: distribution into a customer base that's already paid for software and already trusts a vendor.
- **Risk**: ERP vendors may eventually try to build what we built. Move faster than they can org-chart.

### Tier 3 — Trade associations

- **NTMA** (National Tooling and Machining Association), **PMA** (Precision Metalforming), **AMT** (Association For Manufacturing Technology), **SME**, **AMBA**, **MTConnect Institute**, regional **MEP** (Manufacturing Extension Partnership) centers.
- **Posture**: speaking slots, member-only webinars, newsletter columns, chapter visits. Sponsor sparingly and only where founder visibility is high.
- **What they get**: educational content their members actually want (GCA topics, OAP rigor, shift-handoff data benchmarks).
- **What we get**: implicit endorsement, member-list reach, regional credibility.

### Tier 4 — Workforce & education

- **Trade schools** (e.g., regional technical colleges, NIMS-affiliated programs, Project MFG).
- **Apprenticeship.gov registered programs** and state workforce boards.
- **GI Bill / veteran transition** programs — veterans are an underutilized operator pipeline.
- **Trade unions** where culturally appropriate (IAM, USW lodges with machining components).
- **Posture**: free curriculum, free instructor seats, free student credentials. Long-term grant-eligible revenue when programs adopt OAP / GCA at scale.
- **What they get**: a modern credential their students can actually carry to employers; placement signal.
- **What we get**: a steady inbound of credentialed operators feeding the supply side, plus an extremely defensible workforce-policy story for grants and government.

### Tier 5 — Adjacent software & ecosystem

- **The JobLine VS Code G-Code extension and machine relay** (sibling repos) are part of our own ecosystem — treat them as first-class partners internally and cross-promote.
- **MTConnect-compatible monitoring vendors.** We are not an MES; we benefit from being the human-workflow layer above their data.
- **Quality / inspection software vendors** where CMM data could flow into OAP.
- **Posture**: open integrations, no exclusivity. Avoid co-marketing handcuffs that limit our own positioning.

### Tier 6 — Strategic individual operators

- **YouTube machining creators** with working-shop credibility (covered in doc 03).
- **Industry consultants and shop-floor advisors** with their own client books (e.g., Lean-manufacturing consultants serving job shops).
- **Posture**: long-term relationships, not one-off sponsorships. Bring them into the product roadmap as informal advisors where alignment is real.

## Partnership lifecycle

```text
[Discover]   →  [Pilot]               →  [Formalize]            →  [Co-market]
 Identify       Joint customer / event    MOU or referral terms     Joint content,
 the right      or content collab.        Mutual case study.        joint booth, joint
 contact.      No paperwork yet.                                    PR. Annual review.
```

Don't skip stages. Most "partnerships" that fail were formalized before any joint value was delivered.

## Co-marketing posture

- **Joint case studies** — both logos, both teams quoted, both audiences reached.
- **Joint webinars** — partner audience first, our audience second.
- **Joint conference presence** — share a booth, share a meeting room, share dinners.
- **Joint editorial** — co-bylined trade-pub essays where the angle benefits both.
- **No vague "logo swaps."** Logos on a website without underlying value are noise.

## What we will not do in partnerships

- **No exclusivity that constrains our roadmap.** Especially on integrations.
- **No revenue shares so generous they kill our unit economics.** A friendly referral fee is fine; 30% of ARR forever is not.
- **No partnership announcements before product is ready.** Announcing vapor burns the partner.
- **No partnerships that compromise ITAR posture.** Foreign-owned integration partners get a careful review before any data path opens.

## Strategic partnerships to pursue first (in order)

1. **One Tier-1 tool/metrology vendor** for joint shop-floor integration story. Builds product credibility.
2. **One Tier-2 ERP reseller** in our home region for warm shop intros. Builds sales pipeline.
3. **One Tier-3 association** (NTMA chapter or PMA chapter) for speaking and membership reach. Builds brand.
4. **One Tier-4 trade-school district** for credential rollout. Builds talent supply and the policy story.
5. **One Tier-6 YouTube creator** with genuine product fit. Builds operator awareness.

Five concrete, real partnerships across the five tiers > fifty handshakes that go nowhere.
