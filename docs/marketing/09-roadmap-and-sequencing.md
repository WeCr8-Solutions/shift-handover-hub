# 09 — Roadmap & Sequencing

> **Thesis.** Start compounding plays now (content, credentials, partnerships, founder visibility) even at small budget — they pay off in 6–18 months. Never run more than 3 channels in parallel. Every channel has a written kill rule before it starts. The flagship campaign in doc 10 is sequenced into 180–365-day window once activation and supply density are proven.


## Goal

Put the strategy on a timeline so it's a plan, not a wish list. Sequencing matters as much as choice of tactic; doing the right things in the wrong order wastes a year.

This roadmap is **strategic** — what postures and channels are active in what order, not which feature ships in which sprint.

## Operating principles for sequencing

1. **Compounding plays start earliest.** Content, credentials, partnerships, founder visibility. Even at a small budget. They don't pay off for 6–18 months; start them now or pay later.
2. **Validate channels small before scaling.** Run one cycle (one conference, one partnership, one essay series) before committing budget.
3. **Don't run more than 3 channels in parallel.** Focus beats portfolio at this stage.
4. **Kill criteria are stated up front.** A channel without a kill rule becomes a sunk-cost zombie.
5. **Founder time is the binding constraint, not money.** Plan around the founder's calendar.

## 0–90 days (foundation)

Posture: **Get the canonical assets in place. Have real conversations.**

Strategic priorities:
- **Shop side**: 25+ founder-led shop conversations in the home region. Two paid customers minimum. Two written case studies.
- **Talent side**: GCA + OAP fully live for any operator who finds them. Operator profile flow free of friction. Trade-school pilot conversation started with one regional program.
- **Content**: 10–15 canonical Handbook pages live. llms.txt and structured data clean.
- **Partnerships**: pilot conversations open with one Tier-1 (tool/metrology vendor) and one Tier-2 (ERP reseller).
- **PR**: founder essay submitted to one Tier-1 trade publication.
- **Brand**: founder LinkedIn cadence established (one substantive post per week).

What we are NOT doing yet:
- Paid acquisition at scale.
- Big conference booth presence.
- Hiring a marketing team.
- Press push beyond the one essay.

Kill criteria at day 90:
- Zero in-region paying shops → re-examine ICP or product readiness before continuing.
- < 100 operator signups → talent funnel is broken; fix before any further talent-side spend.

## 90–180 days (validate compounding plays)

Posture: **Confirm which compounding channels are actually compounding. Cut what isn't.**

Strategic priorities:
- **Shop side**: 5+ paid shops total. First customer-referred shop closes. Two-shift trial standardized.
- **Talent side**: First trade-school pilot live. First operator-pulled trial request from an operator at a non-customer shop.
- **Content**: 30+ canonical pages. First long-form founder essay published on a Tier-1 trade outlet.
- **Partnerships**: one Tier-1 partnership formalized at the pilot-customer level. Trade-association chapter visit booked.
- **PR**: first earned trade-press mention. First podcast appearance.
- **Conferences**: attend at least one show small (no booth — just meetings + dinners).

Kill criteria at day 180:
- Trial-to-paid conversion < 40% → freeze acquisition; fix activation.
- No operator-pulled shop interest at all → reassess flywheel design.
- Content showing zero organic traffic growth → either content quality or topic selection is off.

## 180–365 days (scale what's working)

Posture: **Double down on the 2–3 channels that proved out. Add small bets where strategic, not opportunistic.**

Strategic priorities:
- **Shop side**: 20+ paid shops, with at least 30% from referrals or partner intros (the flywheel is starting).
- **Talent side**: 1,000+ credentialed active operators. Second and third trade-school pilots live. First batch of operator-pulled shop conversions.
- **Content**: 75+ canonical pages. First annual State-of-the-Floor data report published.
- **Partnerships**: two formalized partnerships (one Tier-1, one Tier-2 or Tier-3). First joint case study.
- **PR**: 3+ trade-press features. First mainstream-press mention (likely tied to a funding event or the Visibility 100 list).
- **Conferences**: present at one major show in education track. Host one founder dinner at IMTS or Westec.
- **Hiring**: first dedicated GTM hire (likely partnerships or content, not paid acquisition).

Kill criteria at day 365:
- Logo retention at M+6 < 90% → product problem; don't raise on the back of growth that won't compound.
- Talent supply growing >50:1 vs shop demand → over-investment in supply; rebalance.
- Cohort-on-cohort retention degrading → strategic problem; deep quarterly review.

## Channel-by-channel kill criteria

| Channel | Test window | Kill if |
|---|---|---|
| Founder-led shop conversations | Ongoing | Stop only if pipeline conversion drops below 25% for 6 months. Otherwise this never gets cut. |
| Trade-press essays | 12 months | Zero earned features after 4 submitted pitches. Re-examine angles. |
| Conferences | One full year cycle | Zero pipeline within 90 days of a show. Test once more, then drop. |
| Trade-school partnerships | 9 months | No credential rollout traction with one pilot district → reassess curriculum fit. |
| ERP reseller partnerships | 9 months | Zero warm intros delivered → likely wrong partner, try another. |
| YouTube creator sponsorship | One sponsorship cycle | Sub-100 sign-ups attributable → drop. |
| Cold outbound email | 60 days | Sub-1% reply rate on in-ICP targeting → stop. |
| Paid search | Always-on, capped | If non-defensive spend doesn't pay back inside 12 months → keep brand-protect only. |
| Cold paid social | Never started | Don't start at this stage. Revisit at >$5M ARR. |

## When to revisit this roadmap

- **Quarterly.** Walk through priorities, kill criteria, and channel status. Adjust.
- **After any funding event.** New capital changes the binding constraint; sequencing should follow.
- **After a strategic miss** (lost a marquee customer, lost a critical partnership, lost a key hire). Re-plan; don't muscle through.
- **After a strategic win bigger than expected.** Don't blow the momentum by running the old plan.

## What "done" looks like for this library

This roadmap, the metrics in doc 08, and the positioning in doc 00 should be re-readable in 12 months and still describe a recognizable version of the company. If after a year any of those three documents are unrecognizable, we drifted — that's worth a board-level conversation.

The strategy is the company we're trying to be. Tactics change weekly. The strategy shouldn't.
