# 06 — PR & Community

## Goal

Be the brand the trade press, conference organizers, and shop-floor communities already think of when "shift handoff," "operator credentials," or "digital expeditor" comes up.

PR doesn't replace product. It compounds when there's something real to talk about.

## Press strategy

### Vertical trade press first, mainstream press second

The audience that buys is reached by trade press. Spend most PR effort here, even though it doesn't generate VC chatter.

**Primary outlets:**
- *Modern Machine Shop* (Gardner)
- *Production Machining* (Gardner)
- *Cutting Tool Engineering*
- *Manufacturing Engineering* (SME)
- *American Machinist*
- *MetalForming* (PMA)
- *Aerospace Manufacturing and Design*
- Regional manufacturing publications (e.g., *Industry Week*, *Manufacturing.net*, regional MEP newsletters)

**Secondary outlets (mainstream / business):**
- *WSJ* manufacturing desk
- *Bloomberg* industrials
- *The Information* (vertical SaaS angle)
- *TechCrunch* / *Axios Pro* only for funding milestones

### What we pitch (and what we don't)

Pitch:
- Customer outcomes with numbers (% reduction in lost handoff time, hours saved per shift).
- Founder essays on the expeditor problem, ERP coexistence, operator credentials.
- The Manufacturing Visibility 100 list (annual or semi-annual editorial product).
- Operator-of-the-month profiles sourced from the talent roster.
- Industry takes on policy moments (reshoring, tariffs, workforce shortages) — only when timely and substantive.

Don't pitch:
- Feature launches as "news." Trade press doesn't care.
- Funding rounds as the lead story. Funding is a footnote to the substance.
- AI-as-headline. The story is the trade, not the model.
- Vague "transformation" themes.

### Press hygiene

- **One designated press contact** with a real email, not `press@`.
- **Pre-built press kit** (PDF, hosted): one-pager, founder bio + headshot, customer logos with permission, product screenshots, ITAR/security one-pager.
- **Embargo discipline.** Honor embargoes; don't break them for marginal early coverage.
- **No exclusives that aren't worth it.** Exclusives lock out the rest of the trade for a small bump.

## Conference posture

### Show up small with intent, not big with a booth

A $40K booth at IMTS is a write-off for an early-stage company. Spend the same money on:
- Meeting room or co-shared booth space.
- Hosted dinners with 8–12 target shops in town for the show.
- Founder speaking slots in education tracks.
- Roving floor presence in work-appropriate clothing (covered in doc 01).

### Priority shows (US)

| Show | Why |
|---|---|
| **IMTS (Chicago, biennial)** | The trade's center of gravity. Required attendance even if small. |
| **Westec (LA)** | Home-region warm intros; manageable size. |
| **PMTS / Amerimold** | Tighter ICP overlap than the big floors. |
| **Top Shops Conference (Gardner)** | Owner-heavy audience; ideal sales context. |
| **NTMA Annual / PMA Annual** | Association credibility play. |
| **FABTECH** | Fabrication-adjacent shops; secondary ICP. |

### Avoid

- Big SaaS shows (SaaStr, etc.) where the audience doesn't buy.
- Pay-to-pitch "startup village" zones at industry shows — signals desperation.
- Sponsoring lanyards. Money for prestige, not pipeline.

## Community-building philosophy

The trade already has communities. Join them. Don't build a competing one until forced.

### Where to show up

- **Practical Machinist forums.** Long-running, deeply credible. Founder participates under real name, no marketing fluff.
- **r/Machinists, r/CNC, r/Manufacturing.** Same rules. Helpful posts only; product mentions only when directly relevant and disclosed.
- **YouTube comments on creator channels.** Be the person who answers thoughtfully, not the brand that promotes.
- **LinkedIn shop-owner and machinist groups.** Long-form essays from the founder; minimal company posting.
- **In-person regional NTMA / PMA chapter meetings.** Show up, listen, buy dinner.

### When to build our own community surface

Only when:
- We have a category-defining annual event (e.g., a Visibility 100 awards moment, an OAP credential summit) that doesn't exist elsewhere.
- We can sustain it for 5+ years.
- It doesn't compete with partner-association events.

A failed community surface is worse than no community surface.

## Founder visibility (re-emphasized from doc 01)

- **Long-form essays under founder byline**, 1–2 per quarter, on trade pubs and LinkedIn.
- **Podcast appearances** on manufacturing-focused shows (e.g., *Machine Shop Mastery*, *MakingChips*, *The American Manufacturing Podcast*). 4–8 per year.
- **Conference talks**, 4–6 per year, weighted toward education tracks over keynotes.
- **Shop visit content**, regular. Photos and short clips. Not produced — observed.

## Awards and editorial products we run ourselves

- **Manufacturing Visibility 100** — already live. Maintain rigor; refresh ranking transparently; cover honorees with care.
- **Operator of the Month / Shop of the Month** — small, free, sincere. Drives both supply and demand sides.
- **Annual State-of-the-Floor report** — aggregated, anonymized data from the platform (handoff completion rates, shift coverage, credential adoption). Publish for free. This becomes the artifact trade press cites for years.

## What we don't do in PR / community

- **No fake operator accounts.** Ever.
- **No paid placements disguised as editorial.** Trade press will blacklist us if caught.
- **No drive-by community participation that's secretly a marketing campaign.** Operators smell it instantly.
- **No corporate ghost-written posts.** Founder posts in their own voice or doesn't post.
- **No PR around customers without explicit written approval.** Especially ITAR-controlled ones.

## Crisis posture

If a security incident, ITAR misstep, or major outage happens:
- Acknowledge fast, in plain language, under the founder's name.
- Tell affected customers before telling the press.
- Publish a real post-mortem within two weeks.
- Don't oversell the fix. Don't blame vendors. Don't lawyer the language.

The trade respects honesty about hard moments more than it respects polish.
