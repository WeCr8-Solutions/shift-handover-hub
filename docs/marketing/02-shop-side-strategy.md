# 02 — Shop-Side Strategy (Demand)

> **Thesis.** Shops buy from peers, vendors they already pay, and operators they respect — in that order. Founder-led conversations and partner-led intros (doc 05) outperform every other channel by 10x at this stage. Paid acquisition is at the bottom of the priority stack on purpose. **12-month success looks like**: 20+ paid shops, ≥ 30% sourced via referral or partner intro, ≥ 90% logo retention at month 6.


## Goal

Earn the trust of small-to-mid US precision shops fast enough that the second customer comes from the first customer's referral, not from our outbound.

## ICP recap

10–100 employees, multi-shift, regulated end-markets (aerospace, defense, medical), AS9100/ISO 9001/ITAR. Owner or GM is the decision-maker. See `00-positioning.md` for full definition.

## How shop owners actually buy

They don't buy from ads. They buy from:

1. **A peer they trust** told them it works.
2. **A vendor they already pay** (tooling rep, ERP rep, calibration lab, MEP advisor) mentioned it.
3. **They saw it running** at another shop's floor during a visit or audit.
4. **An operator they respect** asked them to install it.

Our channel strategy follows that order. Cold paid acquisition is at the bottom of the priority list, not the top.

## Channel posture (in priority order)

### Tier 1 — Compounding, founder-led

- **Direct shop visits.** Founder-on-the-floor in target regions (SoCal first, then Midwest, then Texas). One visit produces three things: a possible customer, a testimonial, and stories that fuel content. Goal posture: "I want to learn what's hard about your floor," not "I want to demo."
- **Customer-led referrals.** Every paying shop gets asked, "Who else should I be talking to?" within 30 days. Warm intros convert orders of magnitude better than cold.
- **Vendor cross-referrals.** Tooling distributors, calibration labs, ZOLLER reps, ERP implementation consultants. They visit shops weekly. If they trust us, they mention us.

### Tier 2 — Trust-building, indirect

- **Trade associations.** NTMA, PMA, AMT, regional MEP centers. Speaking slots, chapter visits, association newsletters, member-only webinars.
- **Trade press editorial.** Modern Machine Shop, Cutting Tool Engineering, Production Machining. Pitch founder essays and customer case studies, not product launches.
- **Conferences (in person).** IMTS, Westec, Top Shops, regional MEP events. Show up small with intent, not big with a booth (covered in doc 06).
- **Operator-pulled adoption.** When operators discover free GCA/OAP/profiles, they ask their employer to install the shop product. Supply side feeds demand side — see doc 03.

### Tier 3 — Scalable, lower-trust

- **Content + SEO.** Long-tail trade content (covered in doc 04). Compounds over years, not weeks.
- **Outbound email.** Targeted, plain-text, founder-signed, never sequence-bombed. Bias to quality over volume.
- **Paid search.** Defensive only — protect brand terms and a small set of high-intent ERP-comparison queries.

### Tier 4 — Generally avoid

- **Cold paid social.** Shop owners are not on TikTok in their professional capacity.
- **Display/programmatic.** Wrong context, wrong audience.
- **Generic SaaS PR (TechCrunch et al.).** Wrong room. Funding announcements only.
- **Mass cold outbound with sequences.** Burns the domain, burns the brand, doesn't convert this audience.

## Trust-building levers (the things that earn the order)

Shop owners need to believe four things before they'll sign:

1. **You understand my work.** Demonstrated by language, references, and the founder's ability to talk through a setup sheet without flinching.
2. **My data is safe.** ITAR posture, US hosting story, no data leaving customer org. Make this loud on the website and in conversations.
3. **My operators won't reject it.** This is the single biggest objection. Show operator-side testimonials. Offer a two-shift trial.
4. **It won't blow up my ERP.** "Read-through, no replacement" framing. Pre-built JobBOSS and SAP connectors as proof.

Every shop-side surface (website, deck, demo flow, sales call) should address all four in the first five minutes.

## Buyer journey

```text
[Awareness]            [Consideration]                [Decision]               [Expansion]
  └─ Peer mention        └─ Founder conversation        └─ Two-shift trial       └─ More stations
  └─ Trade pub article   └─ Customer reference call     └─ Owner + lead op sign  └─ Multi-site
  └─ Operator request    └─ Live demo on their data     └─ ERP connector live    └─ Talent hiring
  └─ Conference booth    └─ Pricing + ITAR review                                └─ OAP rollout
```

Sales cycle target: **30–60 days** from first conversation to paid trial for the primary ICP. Anything dragging past 90 days is a no — disqualify and revisit in 6 months.

## Pricing posture (strategic, not tactical)

- **Land cheap, expand on value.** Single-station tier exists to remove price as a first-call objection.
- **Per-seat in line with what owners already pay for ERP modules.** Don't anchor higher than their ERP per-user cost.
- **Annual prepay discount.** Cash matters; commitment matters more.
- **Concierge / activation fee for shops > 50 employees.** They expect to pay for onboarding; offering it free signals "small and unproven."

## Trust assets to maintain

- Two-three written customer case studies (PDF + web). Refresh annually.
- One short video per case study (under 90 seconds, real shop, real operator).
- Live reference list of 5+ shops willing to take a phone call.
- ITAR / security one-pager, signed by founder.
- ERP-comparison and ERP-coexistence one-pagers for the top three ERPs in our ICP.

## Trip-wires (when to stop and rethink)

- If we are signing shops only because they want talent hiring (not handoff), the handoff product is failing — fix it before scaling demand-side spend.
- If trial-to-paid conversion drops below 40%, the activation flow is broken; don't pour more leads in.
- If churn at month 6+ is above 5% monthly, fix product before fundraising bigger.
