# 03 — Ranking Methodology

The Manufacturing Visibility 100 ranks people, shops, educators, builders, and companies pushing manufacturing forward. The list rewards practical impact, not follower count.

## Eligible nominees

CNC programmers, machinists, manufacturing engineers, quality leaders, shop owners, tooling experts, automation integrators, manufacturing educators, technical content creators, MES / software builders, robotics and industrial-AI builders, and workforce-development leaders.

## Categories

1. Shop-Floor Innovators
2. CNC and CAM Leaders
3. Manufacturing Educators
4. Small and Mid-Size Shop Leaders
5. Automation and Robotics Leaders
6. Tooling and Metrology Leaders
7. Manufacturing Software Builders
8. Workforce Development Leaders
9. Rising Manufacturing Professionals
10. Legacy Builders
11. Industry Catalysts — Leaders whose platforms, capital, or public influence move manufacturing forward indirectly: AI compute, semiconductors, space and EV scale-up, supply-chain operating systems, foundational tooling. Inclusion in this bucket explicitly acknowledges that the impact is adjacent rather than on the shop floor.

## Scoring model — 100 points total

| Weight | Component | What it measures |
|---:|---|---|
| 25 | **Practical manufacturing impact** | Real shop-floor outcomes: throughput, setup, tooling, inspection, programming, helping SMB shops operate better. |
| 20 | **Modernization / innovation** | Automation, digital work instructions, MES adoption, tool management, robotics, AI-assisted workflows, better data capture. |
| 20 | **Public visibility / industry voice** | LinkedIn, YouTube, podcasts, speaking, published technical content, community engagement. |
| 15 | **Education / mentorship / community** | Teaching, training programs, apprenticeships, public knowledge contribution. |
| 10 | **SMB relevance** | Applicability to small and mid-size shops, not just primes. |
| 10 | **Momentum (trailing 12 months)** | Demonstrable progress in the last year. |

## Editorial guardrails

These rules are non-negotiable. They protect the list's credibility and the people on it.

1. **Human editor review is mandatory** before any rank is published. No published rank derives solely from automated scoring.
2. **No defamation.** We do not publish accusations, insults, or character claims about anyone — featured, omitted, or otherwise.
3. **No private-claim ranking.** Scores rely on publicly verifiable evidence (linked content, public talks, published outcomes).
4. **No pay-for-placement.** Sponsors may underwrite category awards or the surrounding media; sponsorship never buys a rank.
5. **Opt-out is honored immediately.** Any nominee can request to be removed; we comply within 7 days, no questions.
6. **Conflicts of interest are disclosed.** Anyone with a JobLine.ai commercial relationship is labeled.
7. **Year-over-year rank movement is explained,** never implied as a personal verdict.
8. **Nomination is not admission.** Being nominated does not guarantee inclusion or implicate the nominator.

## Output expectations

Each entry includes: name, role/company, category, short citation (why they're on the list), public links, rank, and movement indicator (Year 2+).

## Process notes

- Nominations open continuously; editorial freezes the candidate set 6 weeks before publish.
- An editor pairs each top-50 entry with at least two pieces of public evidence.
- The published list is timestamped and versioned; corrections are issued with a visible changelog.
